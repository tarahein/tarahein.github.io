Tara Hein is a Ph.D. student in the Department of Government at Harvard University. Her research examines citizen–state relations in Latin America. Specifically, she is interested in how perceptions of state failure drive disengagement from democratic institutions or support for undemocratic alternatives. She focuses on high-performing democracies where support for democracy has declined despite strong institutions.

Originally from Monteverde, Costa Rica, Tara holds a BA in Political Science with Interdisciplinary Honors in Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law from Stanford University. Before Harvard, she served as Democracy Impact Coordinator at VélezReyes+, a philanthropic platform working to strengthen democratic governance across the region.

Research

Interests: Citizen–state relations; democratic attitudes; political behavior; perceptions of state performance; Latin America.

In‑progress projects:

  • Perceived State Failure and Democratic Disengagement — survey experiments on how performance narratives shape democratic participation and support.
  • Trust, Services, and Satisfaction in High‑Capacity Democracies — how frontline service experiences update political attitudes.
  • Information Environments & Illiberal Appeal — media frames, insecurity, and support for undemocratic alternatives.

Research & Field Experience

  • Research Assistant, Poverty, Violence, and Governance Lab, Stanford University (2022–2023). Multivariate/time‑series analysis on epidemiological data from 2,557 Mexican municipalities; survey design and RCT analysis in Ecatepec, Mexico.

  • Summer Fellow, Observatorio de Desarrollo y Promoción Social (Mexico, 2021). Evaluated citizen councils; qualitative analysis in NVivo.
  • Summer Fellow & Research Assistant, Stanford Center on Poverty and Inequality (2020–2021). 50+ interviews; mixed‑methods study of everyday life during COVID‑19; selected as 1 of 2 undergraduate TAs for PUBLPOL 141.
