Citizen–state relations in Latin America

Hi, I’m Tara. I’m a Ph.D. student and Presidential Scholar in the Department of Government at Harvard University. My research examines citizen–state relations in Latin America, focusing on how perceptions of state failure shape democratic attitudes and political behavior. I’m particularly interested in the causes and consequences of waning support for democracy in contexts with historically high-performing institutions, like Chile, Costa Rica, and Uruguay.

I received my B.A. in Political Science with Distinction and Interdisciplinary Honors in Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law from Stanford University. My award-winning honors thesis, “Seeing Like a Citizen: Fragmented Citizenship in Santiago de Chile,” examined how socioeconomically vulnerable individuals conceptualize and exercise their citizenship.

Before graduate school, I was the Democracy Impact Coordinator at the Latin American philanthropic organization VélezReyes+, where I developed a network of political leadership incubators across 7 countries: Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Peru, and Uruguay.

I am currently a research affiliate at the Poverty, Violence, and Governance Lab and the Democracy Action Lab, both at Stanford University’s Center on Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law.

You can reach me at tarahein@g.harvard.edu.
