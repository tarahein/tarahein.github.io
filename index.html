Citizen–state relations in Latin America

Hi, I’m Tara. I’m a Ph.D. student and Presidential Scholar in the Department of Government at Harvard University. My research examines citizen–state relations in Latin America, focusing on how perceptions of state failure shape democratic attitudes and political behavior. I’m particularly interested in why democratic backsliding occurs even in high-performing institutional contexts.

Before graduate school, I was the Democracy Impact Coordinator at the philanthropic organization VélezReyes+, where I helped develop political leadership incubators in Costa Rica, Colombia, Peru, Brazil, Chile, Argentina, and Uruguay. I also supported the design of a randomized controlled trial assessing how managerial and democratic training influences mayoral performance in Peru.

I received my B.A. in Political Science with Distinction and Interdisciplinary Honors in Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law from Stanford University. My honors thesis, “Seeing Like a Citizen: Fragmented Citizenship in Santiago de Chile,” explored how perceptions of state performance affect democratic legitimacy.

I am currently an External Affiliate of the Poverty, Violence, and Governance Lab and a Graduate Student Affiliate of the Democracy Action Lab, both at Stanford University’s Center on Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law.

You can reach me at tarahein@g.harvard.edu.
