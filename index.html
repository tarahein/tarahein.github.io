Welcome!

Hi, I’m Tara. I’m a Ph.D. student and Presidential Scholar in the Department of Government at Harvard University. My research examines citizen–state relations in Latin America, focusing on how perceptions of state failure shape democratic attitudes and political behavior.

You can find a copy of my CV here.

I received my B.A. in Political Science with Distinction and Interdisciplinary Honors in Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law from Stanford University. My award-winning honors thesis, “Seeing Like a Citizen: Fragmented Citizenship in Santiago de Chile,” received the Firestone Medal for Excellence in Undergraduate Research.

I am currently a research affiliate at the Poverty, Violence, and Governance Lab and the Democracy Action Lab at Stanford University’s Center on Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law.
